Recall that $sin(x)$ is an odd function and $cos(x)$ is an even function. That is,
\begin{center}
    $sin(-x) = -sin(x)$ \\
    $cos(-x) = cos(x)$ \\
\end{center}

Negating $x$ in Euler's formula therefore yields:

\begin{center}
    $e^{-ix} = isin(-x) + cos(-x)$ \\
    $e^{-ix} = -isin(x) + cos(x)$ \\
    $e^{-ix} = cos(x) -isin(x) $ \\
                and \\ 
    $-e^{-ix} = isin(x) - cos(x)$
\end{center}
